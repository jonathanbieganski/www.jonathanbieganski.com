About Me

Hello, my name is Jonathan Bieganski folks call me JB. I want to thank you for taking an interest in me.

My career started in 2005 as a member of the Microservices team at Computer Assiociates, which is now known as CA Technologies. The Microservices Manager saw something in me and gave me the opportunity of a lifetime; start out my career working for a large software company. (Thank you, Bill.)

The first professional internal tool I had ever created was written at CA Technologies and was written in Visual basic. That was also the first time I had written a program outside of academia and more importantly, the first time I saw a tool I created used by others. I received professional feedback and made improvements to the tool. From that moment on I hooked by the creativity and utility coding facilitates. After the housing market crash of 2008 and the extensive layoffs witnessed in 2010, I took the expereince I gained at CA Technologies and ventured forth to apply my skills elsewhere.

Between 2010 and 2012 I was a contractor troubleshooting and standing up systems. I landed an Instructional Design role at City College of San Francisco where I had been attending Computer Science and Ethical hacking courses. I'll never forget dropping off my resume in front of the Technology Learning Center Staff, introducing myself and being dismissed only to have my future managers briskly walk and stop me down the hallway, I assume after scanning my resume. I trained Professors how to create courses in Canvas and Moodle. I trained Lad Aides and purchased hardware and maintained the systems in the lab. I learned a lot about academia and thier unique needs and challenges. (Thank You, Vic and Carol)

I left school to care for an ailing family member and picked up a few projects troubleshooting systems, setting up networks and security systems. Around this time I met an amazing EdTech Sales Manager and eventually made her my wife. She motivated me to continue my pursuetes and my dream to work Silicon Valley. Eventually the Lead Systems Administrator at icontrol Networks, which was absorbed by Alarm.com, saw something in me and brought me in under his wing. (Thank you, Mike.) I learned about Tier 1 server updates, Atlassian Suite permissions, print management, VOIP and Cisco managed switch configuration. I also completed my very first Microsoft Certification: Enabling and Managing M365 (M20347). Working at Alarm.com and living directly behind Oricals headquaters in a tiny overproced apartment was a dream fulfilled for me. If I could have entertained purchasing a home and building equity near Silicon Valley with what I was making, I would still be there.

With extensive experience in the tech industry, I specialize in Azure Cloud Services and automated solutions. I've worked in diverse corporate settings. Please look around and feel free to contact and connect with me.
