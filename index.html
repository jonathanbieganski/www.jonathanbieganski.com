About Me

Thank you for taking an interest.

My tech career began in 2005 at CA Technologies on their Microservices team. There, I created my first professional internal tool in Visual Basic, sparking my passion for problem-solving and coding.

After the 2008 housing market crash, I broadened my skills troubleshooting systems and networks. I was studying computer science when I transitioned to an Instructional Design role at City College of San Francisco. I trained professors and managed the lab. This experience taught me many valuable lessons about academia's unique needs and challenges.

I briefly stepped away from my career to care for a family member, taking on tech projects when possible. It was during this time that I met an amazing EdTech Sales Manager. I eventually convinced her to marry me and she convinced me to follow my dream to work in Silicon Valley.

The Lead Systems Administrator at iControl Networks recognized my potential and brought me on board. There I gained experience in various systems and earned my first Microsoft Certification. Alarm.com absorded icontrol networks. I began to honed my skills and specialize in Azure, M365, automation, server management and more. The high cost of living in Silicon Valley led me to resign and relocate to Sacramento where lined up a junior System Administrator position at Magnit.

The IT Director at RSN Technologies reached out and offered me an IT Manager / System Administrator role. I managed a small team and handled various IT responsibilities. At the beginning of the covid pandemic, when lockdowns began and circumstances changed, I sold my Sacramento home and moved to Oregon.

I started work at Fisher Phillips. There, my role is varied. I manage Microsoft System Center Configuration Manager & Microsoft Endpoint Configuration Manager, Manage projects, stand up ITCM solutions, write scripts, create workflows and automations.

With a diverse background in tech and a specialization in Microsoft Azure Cloud Services and M365 Stack, I'm open to connecting and discussing new opportunities.
