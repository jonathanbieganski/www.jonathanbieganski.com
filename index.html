About Me

Thank you for taking an interest.

My tech career began in 2005 at CA Technologies on their Microservices team, thanks to an opportunity from the Microservices Manager Bill. There, I created my first professional internal tool in Visual Basic, sparking my passion for coding and problem-solving.

After the 2008 housing market crash, I broadened my skills as a contractor, troubleshooting systems and networks. I transitioned to an Instructional Design role at City College of San Francisco, training professors and managing lab systems. This experience taught me many valuable lessons about academia's unique needs and challenges.

I briefly stepped away from my career to care for a family member, taking on troubleshooting and networking projects when possible. It was during this time that I met an amazing EdTech Sales Manager. I convinced her to marry me and she convinced me to follow my dream of working in Silicon Valley.

The Lead Systems Administrator at iControl Networks recognized my potential and brought me on board. There I gained experience in various areas and obtained my first Microsoft Certification. icontrol networks was absorbed by Alarm.com, where I honed my skills in Azure, M365, server management, VOIP, and more. The high cost of living in Silicon Valley led me to resign from Alarm.com and relocate to Sacramento where I became a Junior System Administrator at Magnit.

The IT Director at RSN Technologies offered a System Administrator role where I managed a team and handled various IT responsibilities, especially during the beginning challenges of the Covid pandemic. When circumstances changed, I sold my Sacramento home, moved to Oregon, and began working for Fisher Phillips. There, I've taken on roles such as managing Microsoft System Center Configuration Manager, implementing Intune and automating processes.

With a diverse background in tech and a specialization in Microsoft Azure Cloud Services and M365, I'm open to connecting and discussing new opportunities.
