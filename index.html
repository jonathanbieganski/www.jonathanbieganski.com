About Me

Thank you for taking an interest in me today and thank you to each and every person that either hired me or had a role in my hiring. Without your contribution to my career I would not be the person I am today.

My career started in 2005 as a member of the Microservices team at Computer Assiociates, which is now known as CA Technologies. The Microservices Manager saw something in me and gave me the opportunity of a lifetime; start out my career working for a large software company. (Thank you, Bill.) The first professional internal tool I had ever created was written at CA Technologies and was written in Visual basic. That was also the first time I had written a program outside of academia and more importantly, the first time I saw a tool I created used by others. I received professional feedback and made improvements to the tool. From that moment on I hooked by the creativity and utility coding facilitates. After the housing market crash of 2008 and the extensive layoffs witnessed in 2010, I took the expereince I gained at CA Technologies and ventured forth to apply my skills elsewhere.

Between 2010 and 2012 I was a contractor troubleshooting and standing up systems. I landed an Instructional Design role at City College of San Francisco where I had been attending Computer Science and Ethical hacking courses. I'll never forget dropping off my resume in front of the Technology Learning Center Staff, introducing myself and being dismissed only to have my future managers briskly walk and stop me down the hallway, I assume after scanning my resume. I trained Professors how to create courses in Canvas and Moodle. I trained Lad Aides and purchased hardware and maintained the systems in the lab. I learned a lot about academia and thier unique needs and challenges. (Thank You, Vic and Carol)

To care for an ailing family member, I resigned my role and coursework. I picked up a few projects troubleshooting systems, setting up networks and security systems. Around this time I met an amazing EdTech Sales Manager and eventually convinced her to marry me. (Thank you, Amanda.) Amande motivated me to get back into tech field proper and continue my dream to work and experience Silicon Valley. A Lead Systems Administrator at icontrol Networks, which was absorbed by Alarm.com, saw something in me and brought me in under him. (Thank you, Mike.) I learned about Second Tuesday Microsoft Server updates, Atlassian Suite permissions, print management, VOIP and Cisco managed switch configuration. I also completed my very first Microsoft Certification: Enabling and Managing M365 (M20347). Working at Alarm.com and living directly behind Oricals headquaters in a tiny overproced apartment was a dream fulfilled. With that said, if I could have entertained purchasing a home and building equity anywhere near Silicon Valley, I would still be at Alarm.com.

We found our first home near Sacramento Calfornia where I had successfully acquired a role as a Junior System Administrator at Magnit. (Thank you, Robert.) I wrote scripts, managed the Microsoft, Mimecast and Sophos portals. Home ownership is expensive and an opportunity to become as a full fledged System Administrator was available at RSN Technologies. (Thank you, Charlie.) While at RSN, I managed a team of five ServiceDesk members, interviewed candidates, managed multiple Microsoft Tenants, implemented MDM solutions and handled the occasional escalations. RSN specialized in managing services for nursing homes. Covid was an especially difficult time for us and our clients. As early days of Covid and lockdowns started my service at RSN were no longer warranted.

During the first California lockdown we decided to put our house on the market and look for property with some land. We managed to sell our home in Sacremento fairly quickly and shortly after moved into our new rural property outside Portland, OR. I interviewed with my current employer Fisher Phillips shortly after arriving in Oregon. Once hired, I directly reported to the Director of Information Technology. (Thank you, Michael & Joey) I started my career at Fisher Phillips by taking over Microsoft System Center Configuration Manager from the Systems Team, migrated away from and sunseting ServiceNow and created a few PowerAutomate workflows. Recently I have been living in Microsoft Endpoint Configuration Manager implementing Intune for our many entities.

Thank you for taking a look at my about section. With extensive experience in the tech industry i've worked in diverse corporate settings. I've recently specialization in Microsoft Azure Cloud Services. Please feel free to contact and connect with me.
